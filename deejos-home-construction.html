What is the Process of Getting Building Plan Sanction in Chennai?

Your Comprehensive Guide to Obtaining Building Plan Approval in Chennai

At DEEJOS, we understand the importance of getting it right, and we're dedicated to making the process as smooth as possible for you. So, let's dive in and explore everything you need to know about obtaining the necessary permissions and approvals in Chennai.

Authority for Approval: Understanding CMDA Regulations In Chennai, the Chennai Metropolitan Development Authority (CMDA) is the regulatory body responsible for granting planning permissions under the Tamil Nadu Town and Country Planning Act 1971. These permissions are valid for three years from the date of issue.

The CMDA delegates powers to local bodies within the Chennai Metropolitan Area (CMA) for approving planning permissions for various categories of construction. For major developments like high-rise buildings, CMDA directly issues planning permissions.

Applying for Planning and Building Permits: A Step-by-Step Process The application process for planning and building permits in Chennai follows a structured approach. Here's a simplified breakdown:

  • Registration and Form Submission: Applicants must register on the citizen portal of the local body and submit the required forms online. These forms include applications for planning permissions and undertakings from the applicant/owner.
  • Selection of Registered Professionals: Applicants choose registered architects or engineers who will prepare the necessary drawings and submit the applications for approval.
  • Document Verification and Auto-Scrutiny: The registered professional visits the site, collects documents, and prepares drawings. These are then submitted online for auto-scrutiny to ensure compliance with building regulations.
  • Site Inspection: An Assistant Executive Engineer (AEE) conducts a site visit within seven days of application submission. The inspection verifies various aspects such as site boundaries, access roads, and zoning.
  • Document Verification by AEE: The AEE verifies the submitted documents. If additional details are required, the applicant must provide them within 15 days.
  • Approval Process: Once all documents are in order, the application is submitted to the Executive Engineer (EE) for approval. If approved, a demand advice is generated, and the applicant is notified to make the necessary payment.
  • Fee Payment and Final Approval: Upon payment verification, the EE approves the fees, and the final building permit is generated. The applicant receives approval via SMS and email and can download the certificate online.

For non-high-rise buildings, the following documents are typically required:

  • Application forms
  • Undertakings from the applicant/owner
  • Copy of property documents
  • Affidavits of undertaking
  • Revenue records
  • Encumbrance certificate
  • NOCs from relevant authorities

Additionally, specific documentation may be required for buildings in regulated areas, coastal regulation zones, and hill conservation areas.
